Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Baby-Led Weaning (BLW) and spoon-feeding?

BLW encourages babies to self-feed soft, finger-sized strips of food from the start, focusing on motor skill development and self-regulation. Traditional weaning typically starts with purees, though many parents successfully use a hybrid approach that incorporates both textures.

When is it recommended to start introducing solid foods?

Most major health organizations recommend beginning solid food introduction around 6 months of age, when your baby shows clear developmental signs of readiness. These signs include holding their head up independently, sitting securely with minimal support, showing curiosity toward your food, and losing the tongue-thrust reflex that pushes objects out of their mouth.

How is my tracking progress saved? Will it disappear when I close the app?

Your weaning checklist data logs are automatically saved directly onto your device using secure browser technology called local storage. This means you can freely close your tabs, shut down your web browser, or completely restart your mobile phone without losing any checked foods or milestone markers. When you return to the website, your list will restore exactly where you left it.
